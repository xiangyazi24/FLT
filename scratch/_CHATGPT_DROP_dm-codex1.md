# Q: FLT N12 2-isogeny boundary audit

## Audit verdict

The proposed 2-isogeny route is mathematically sound, but only after adding the standard support and exact-sequence hypotheses. The key correction is that the rank formula uses exact Kummer image sizes, not merely visible witnesses, and the denominator is `#F(Q)[2] = 4`.

For

\[
F:Y^2=X^3+2X^2-3X=X(X-1)(X+3),
\]

with kernel generated by `(0,0)`, the dual is

\[
F':Y^2=X^3-4X^2+16X.
\]

The audited image claims are correct:

\[
\operatorname{im}\alpha_F=\{1,-1,3,-3\},\qquad
\operatorname{im}\alpha_{F'}=\{1\}.
\]

## 1. Missing hypotheses in the 2-isogeny rank formula

Use the standard model

\[
G:y^2=x^3+ax^2+bx,
\]

with rational kernel point `(0,0)`. The descent map is

\[
\alpha_G(O)=1,\qquad \alpha_G((0,0))=b,\qquad \alpha_G(x,y)=x\pmod{\mathbf Q^{*2}}\quad(x\ne0).
\]

For `F`, `a=2`, `b=-3`; for `F'`, `a'=-4`, `b'=16`.

The formula requires:

1. `F` and `F'` are nonsingular over `Q`, characteristic not `2`.
2. The isogenies `φ : F → F'` and `φ̂ : F' → F` are dual and satisfy `φ̂ ∘ φ = [2]`.
3. The maps `alpha_F` and `alpha_F'` are the connecting maps with
   \[
   \ker\alpha_F=\hat\phi(F'(Q)),\qquad
   \ker\alpha_{F'}=\phi(F(Q)).
   \]
4. `F(Q)[2]` has order `4`; here this holds because the roots are `-3,0,1`.
5. The exact sequence gives
   \[
   \#\operatorname{im}\alpha_F\cdot\#\operatorname{im}\alpha_{F'}
   =\#F(Q)/2F(Q)=2^{\operatorname{rank}F(Q)}\cdot\#F(Q)[2].
   \]

Thus in this case

\[
2^{\operatorname{rank}F(Q)}=rac{4\cdot1}{4}=1.
\]

Do not omit the factor `#F(Q)[2]=4`.

## 2. Audit of `im(alpha') = {1}`

This is correct, but the proof has three separate obligations.

First, prove the support lemma. For any nonzero `X=A/B^2` in lowest square-denominator form on `F'`,

\[
C^2=A(A^2-4AB^2+16B^4),
\]

and

\[
\gcd(A,A^2-4AB^2+16B^4)\mid16.
\]

Therefore the squareclass of `X` is represented by one of

\[
\{1,-1,2,-2\}.
\]

Second, reject the negative classes over `R`. The quartics are

\[
W^2=-U^4-4U^2V^2-16V^4
\]

and

\[
W^2=-2U^4-4U^2V^2-8V^4,
\]

which have no nonzero real projective solution. Equivalently, on `F'`,

\[
X^2-4X+16=(X-2)^2+12>0,
\]

so a real point must have `X ≥ 0`.

Third, reject class `2` over `Q_2`. The homogeneous space is

\[
W^2=2U^4-4U^2V^2+8V^4,\qquad \gcd(U,V)=1.
\]

Modulo `16`:

- `U` odd, `V` even gives RHS `2 mod 16`;
- `U,V` both odd gives RHS `6 mod 16`;
- `U` even, `V` odd gives RHS `8 mod 16`.

No square modulo `16` is `2`, `6`, or `8`; the both-even case is excluded by primitivity. Hence class `2` is impossible. The class `1` survives, e.g. via `O`, `(0,0)`, or the affine point `(4,8)` on `F'`.

So `im(alpha_F')={1}` is justified once the support lemma and primitive-clearing lemma are included.

## 3. Exact finite obligations for a Lean certificate

The later certificate should prove these finite/checkable facts.

### Image of `alpha_F`

Candidate universe:

\[
D_F=\{1,-1,3,-3\}.
\]

Support lemma:

\[
\alpha_F(P)\in D_F\quad\text{for all }P\in F(Q).
\]

Witnesses:

| class | witness on `F` |
|---:|---:|
| `1` | `O` or `(1,0)` |
| `-3` | `(0,0)` by special convention, also `(-3,0)` |
| `-1` | `(-1,2)` |
| `3` | `(3,6)` |

Thus `#im(alpha_F)=4`.

### Image of `alpha_F'`

Candidate universe:

\[
D_{F'}=\{1,-1,2,-2\}.
\]

Support lemma:

\[
\alpha_{F'}(P)\in D_{F'}\quad\text{for all }P\in F'(Q).
\]

Finite rejection data:

| class | certificate |
|---:|---|
| `-1` | no nonzero real solution to `W^2=-U^4-4U^2V^2-16V^4` |
| `-2` | no nonzero real solution to `W^2=-2U^4-4U^2V^2-8V^4` |
| `2` | no primitive solution mod `16` to `W^2=2U^4-4U^2V^2+8V^4` |

Surviving witness:

| class | witness on `F'` |
|---:|---:|
| `1` | `O`, `(0,0)`, or `(4,8)` |

Thus `#im(alpha_F')=1`.

### Rank and torsion obligations

After the image certificates:

1. apply the standard 2-isogeny exact-sequence/rank theorem;
2. prove `rank F(Q)=0`;
3. prove good reduction at `5` and `7`;
4. enumerate finite points:
   \[
   \#F(\mathbf F_5)=8,\qquad \#F(\mathbf F_7)=8;
   \]
5. use torsion injection at good reduction primes to get `#F(Q)_tors ∣ 8`;
6. exhibit the eight rational points
   \[
   O,\ (-3,0),(0,0),(1,0),(-1,\pm2),(3,\pm6).
   \]

Since rank is zero, all rational points are torsion, and the visible eight points exhaust `F(Q)`.

## 4. Clean final theorem boundary for the current N12 file

The current N12 file should depend on a hard boundary theorem in point-list form, not on the descent internals.


For the denominator residual, an even weaker boundary is enough:

\[
E(Q)\ni(x,y)\Longrightarrow x\in\{-2,0,1,2,4\}.
\]

Then the existing primitive square-denominator wrapper is elementary: if `x=A/B^2` is one of those integers and `gcd(A,B)=1`, then `B=1`.

## 5. False shortcuts to avoid

- Do not set `alpha((0,0))=0`; the target is `Q*/Q*2`. The correct special values are `-3` on `F` and `16`, hence `1`, on `F'`.
- Do not infer `im(alpha_F)` from visible points alone; the reverse inclusion needs the support lemma.
- Do not infer `im(alpha_F')={1}` from real and mod-16 obstructions unless the support lemma first restricts candidates to `{1,-1,2,-2}`.
- Do not state the mod-16 obstruction without primitivity of `(U,V)`; otherwise fake all-even residue solutions appear.
- Do not use torsion reduction at one prime as an injection for all torsion. Use good reductions at both `5` and `7` to bound all primary torsion.
