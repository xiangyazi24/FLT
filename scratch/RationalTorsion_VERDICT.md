Also, be careful mathematically: over ℚ, the statement
leanE[n](ℚ) ≃ ZMod n × ZMod n
is false in general. The true statement is over an algebraic closure, with the usual characteristic hypothesis:


$$E[n](\bar{\mathbb Q}) \cong (\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z)^2.$$


For rational torsion you want an injection:


$$E[n](\mathbb Q) \hookrightarrow E[n](\bar{\mathbb Q})
\cong (Z/n)^2.$$

Those are the kind of API you would use for the injection of rational points into points over an extension field. Lean Community
